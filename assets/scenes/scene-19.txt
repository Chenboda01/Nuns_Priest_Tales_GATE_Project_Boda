Chanticleer began his story. "Two friends once went on a journey together. When they arrived at a crowded town, there was only room for one at the inn. The unlucky friend had to sleep in an ox's stall in a faraway yard. That night, the lucky friend had a terrible dream. He saw his companion crying for help: 'I am being murdered in the ox's stall! Save me!' The dream came three times, but each time he thought it was just a dream and went back to sleep. The next morning, he found his friend had been killed for his money — exactly as the dream had warned. The murderers were caught and punished. This proves that dreams can be true warnings!"
